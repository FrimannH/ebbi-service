METHOD

This survey is designed to measure the consumer’s perception towards {{getReportResponse.customerName}} brand. The surveys questions asked are based on elements that identify energy specific brand equity constructs."&" The EBBI (Energy Brand Benchmark Measurement) was created through rigorous academic process, that has been adapted to the energy space. The result for each element is benchmarked to the total mean of nominees in the CHARGE Awards 2016

The survey was conducted among {{getReportResponse.customerName}} customers and took place from {{getReportResponse.reportStartDate}} to {{getReportResponse.reportEndDate}}. Respondents were of both sexes aged {{getReportResponse.ageRangeFrom}} - {{getReportResponse.ageRaneTo}} years. Reminder for participation was sent out twice to those who had not yet answered. Respondents were {{getReportResponse.respondentCount}} in total.

Brief explanations of statistical terms in the report:

Mean is the average value of a set of numbers. It tells you where the central tendency of the answers to a particular question lies. The mean is calculated by simply adding the numeric answers for all participants and dividing them with the total number of participants.

Standard deviation tells you how much the answers to a particular question, on a continuous scale, are spread around its mean. So if the standard deviation to a particular question is low it means that there are little differences in the attitudes of respondents, if however the standard deviation is high it means the differences in the respondent’s attitude is greater.

Chi-square test is a significance test used to determine significant differences in background groups on a non-consecutive response scales.

ANOVA stands for Analysis of Variance and assesses whether there are significant differences in the answers mean to a question on a continuous scale.

Net Promoter Score (NPS) is a mathematical classification of a consumer’s brand loyalty. Usually measured within the scale of 1-10, it provides a simplified, yet highly effective vision of a provider’s brand popularity and share-ability amongst its consumers
The Net Promoter Score Question is simple but the responses collected to this one question could be critical to your organization’s growth strategy. This is especially the case when you are considering the future of your product/service and how it can grow “organically” through word-of-mouth
You are further able to use your customers’ NPS score as a background variable to explore your brand’s results on all the EBBI constructs.
